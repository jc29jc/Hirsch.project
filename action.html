03 // Taking Action

Just talking about a problem doesn't fix it, so this page is all about solutions and what everybody can do. We are going to talk about big national movements and protests trying to change laws behind this problem, and actual groups helping here in Marin County, and finally, showing different simple ways people can help out everyday.

    National Movements & Protests

    Ban the Box

    The ban the box movement supports the idea that job applications shouldn't have questions about former criminal history, so that formerly incarcerated people have the same chance of getting a job as the general public.

    Second Chance Act

    The Second Chance Act (SCA) is the largest source of federal funding for reentry. It provides grants to state and local governments and nonprofits to offer employment training, housing, substance abuse treatment, and mentoring.

    Other Inspiring Movements

    If you want to look into other national efforts, you can check out Blackstone Career Institute's list of inspiring organizations. This page highlights incredible groups like the Prison Fellowship, the Innocence Project, and the Center for Employment Opportunities. It shows how these different groups focus on providing education, legal help, and job training to make sure incarcerated and formerly incarcerated people get the support they need to rebuild their lives.

    Community Networks

    Simple Ways to Help

    Regular citizens can assist by supporting fair-chance employers, volunteering with tutoring or mentorship programs, and spreading accurate information to counter harmful stereotypes.
