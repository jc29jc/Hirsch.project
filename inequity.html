01 // The Inequity

Welcome to the Inequity page. Before we jump into numbers or statistics, we need to understand the human side of this problem. When someone leaves prison, everyday tasks that seem simple to us like finding a good place to sleep or getting a job turn into huge challenges for these ex-convicts. This page will break down the major barriers that returning citizens face the second they get out of prison.

// The Big Problems & Questions

  • 01

    What struggles do people face the moment they get out of prison?

    The Starting Money Gap

    Most people leave prison with almost no money. Since they have no cash and no safety net, they struggle to buy basic things like food and clothes. This poverty forces many people to do many minor survival crimes just to stay alive, leading to them being arrested again.

    Mental Health and a Different World

    Being locked up can cause mental trauma, like anxiety, depression, and a lack of trust in society. On top of that, people who have been away for a long time return to a completely different world. Without any help, they have to figure out how to use things they've never seen before on their own, like phones or etc.

  • 02

    How does having a criminal record ruin someone's chances of finding a place to live or getting a job?

    Job and Housing

    It is almost impossible to build a normal life because landlords and bosses use background checks to throw away applications from anyone with a bad record. This creates a ripple effect. You cannot get a job without a home, but you cannot pay for a home without a job, leaving you hopeless.

    Strict Government Rules

    The government laws are blocking ex-convicts from getting help. For example, strict federal rules can permanently ban people with certain records from living in affordable housing, even though it is exactly what they need to avoid homelessness. This leads them with no safe place to live.

  • 03

    How does society focusing on punishing people instead of helping them change hurt their chances of a fresh start?

    The Ripple Affect

    When a society focuses on punishing people instead of helping them change, it makes it way harder for them to start over and change. This can cause trauma for these people because they are so worried about getting into trouble again, and they also feel hopeless because everyone oppresses them. It makes it super hard for ex-convicts then to find a job or place to live, which no one realizes that these are the real reasons people commit crimes in the first place. People think that constantly locking people up is for a positive change, but later down the road it actually makes people more likely to commit crimes again. Its a big ripple affect.

    No Chance to Improve

    Focusing only on punishment takes away the things that people need to be able to learn from their actions like school, job, and mental health care. Without these different programs, many people are only focused on passing their punishment time rather than being focused on changing. To add on, many people may even leave prison less prepared for the real world because they haven't been in contact with it, which leaves them with having to learn all of the modern stuff on their own and that affects how they are able to change their lives.

  • 04

    How does discrimination against ex-convicts lead to systemic exclusion?

    Always Left Out

    Discrimination against ex-convicts leaves them shut out of society for the rest of their lives. This is because of social norms, where since everyone around us constantly discriminates people with criminal records, the next generation and on will pick up the same thoughts about these people. This causes them to face constant challenges like getting basic needs. This makes it practically impossible to escape poverty which traps them in a cycle where they are unable to find a job, place to live, or fit in with everyone else.
