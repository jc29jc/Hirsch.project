  • 01

    What is the unemployment rate for people with a record compared to everyone else?

    The Job Rate

    According to the Prison Policy Initiative, ex convicts face an unemployment rate of over 27%. In comparison, the general publics rate of unemployment is 4%, which is 5x less then the unemployment rate of ex-convicts. To make it even worse, this level of unemployment is worse than the worst years of the Great Depression. And people might think that this is probably because most ex-convicts don't want to work, but this is not true. Over 93% of released prisoners are looking for jobs compared to 84% of the general public. This bad rate for these prisoners are because ex-convicts face a 50% rate of not getting a job. When they do get a job, they are still discriminated, and earn about 40% less money then their peers.
