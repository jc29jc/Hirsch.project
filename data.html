02 // The Data Hub

Once you know what the everyday struggles are, this page will bring in the numbers and evidence. This is the page where we back up every question and information on The Inequity page with real facts and interesting statistics. We're going to look at the math behind unemployment, how tax money is being spent, and the evidence of how many ex-convicts end up struggling once they get out of jail.

// Empirical Data & Metrics

(Click on a data box below to expand the evidence directly underneath)

  • 01

    Unemployment & Economic Statistics

    The Jobless Rate

    Data shows that the unemployment rate for formerly incarcerated people is around 27%, which is higher than the total U.S. unemployment rate during the Great Depression.

  • 02

    Fiscal Spending & Prison Budgets

    Tax Money vs. Rehabilitation

    Billions of tax dollars go into holding people in prisons, while very little funding is put toward education or job training programs that help people transition back into society safely.

  • 03

    Recidivism Rates

    The Cycle of Rearrest

    Without support networks or structural resources upon release, a high percentage of ex-convicts face extreme poverty, which directly drives them right back into jail within a few years.
